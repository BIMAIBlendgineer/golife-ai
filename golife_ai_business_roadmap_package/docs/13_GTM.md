# 13 - Go-To-Market Strategy

## Launch framing

Do not launch as "the app that does everything."

Launch as:

> the AI that gives you 3 realistic missions every morning.

## Launch promise

In one screen, GoLife should help the user:

- finish one important thing;
- protect one minimum habit;
- avoid one unnecessary spend;
- use one item already at home.

## Initial campaign

### 7-Day GoLife Reset

For 7 days the app helps the user:

- complete 1 important task per day;
- keep 1 minimum habit;
- avoid 1 avoidable expense;
- use 1 pantry item before it is wasted.

## Channels

- TikTok / Reels demo loops
- Product Hunt
- Reddit productivity / frugal / meal-prep communities
- indie hacker and Flutter communities
- email waitlist
- private beta referrals

## Landing page structure

1. Hero: "Your day, turned into 3 realistic missions."
2. Problem: too many disconnected apps.
3. Demo: task + pantry + spend -> mission output.
4. Explanation and privacy.
5. Pricing.
6. Waitlist / beta CTA.

## Beta targets

- 100 waitlist signups
- 30 active private beta users
- 10 qualitative interviews
- 14-day beta usage window

## Launch metrics

- waitlist conversion
- onboarding activation
- D1 / D7 / D30 retention
- missions accepted
- missions completed
- paid conversion
